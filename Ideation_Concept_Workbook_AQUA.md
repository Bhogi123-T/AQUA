# Ideation and Concept Generation Lab
**Completed for: AQUA (Smart Aquaculture Platform)**

## Worksheet 1: SCAMPER Ideation Sheet
**Purpose:** To generate new ideas by applying creative prompts to your core product (the AQUA Platform).
**SCAMPER Worksheet – “Innovating the AQUA Platform”**

| SCAMPER Prompt | Guiding Question | Your Ideas (AQUA Context) |
|---|---|---|
| **S – Substitute** | What materials, colors, or components can I replace? | 1. Substitute text-heavy English menus with voice-assisted, icon-driven vernacular (local language) interfaces.<br>2. Substitute traditional password login with OTP-based mobile login for ease of use. |
| **C – Combine** | Can I merge this product with another function? | 1. Combine predictive AI (disease/yield forecasting) with a direct B2B marketplace so farmers can protect *and* sell in one app.<br>2. Combine weather forecasting APIs with our feeding algorithm to adjust feed automatically based on temperature. |
| **A – Adapt** | Can I borrow ideas from another field? | 1. Adapt "credit scoring" algorithms from fintech to create an "AQUA Trust Score" for farmers based on their pond health history, making them eligible for micro-loans.<br>2. Adapt the "social feed" concept to create a farmer-expert community hub. |
| **M – Modify** | What can I exaggerate, shrink, or simplify? | 1. Simplify the onboarding process so a farmer can register their pond with just 3 clicks.<br>2. Exaggerate the visual alerts—turn the entire screen red when a critical drop in dissolved oxygen is detected. |
| **P – Put to Another Use** | Can it serve an entirely different purpose? | 1. Put the aggregated water quality data to another use: selling regional disease heatmaps to government fisheries departments or insurance companies.<br>2. Use the app as an educational platform for training new farmers. |
| **E – Eliminate** | What can I remove to make it simpler/smarter? | 1. Eliminate the middlemen completely by allowing farmers to auction their harvest directly to verified exporters.<br>2. Eliminate the need for manual data entry by connecting directly to affordable IoT sensors. |
| **R – Reverse / Rearrange** | What happens if I change the order of use? | 1. Reverse the info flow: Instead of farmers searching for disease symptoms, the AI Vision tool proactively identifies it from a photo and pushes the treatment plan to them.<br>2. Rearrange the supply chain: Buyers commit to purchasing *before* the final harvest based on AQUA's yield predictions. |

## Worksheet 2: Concept Card
**Purpose:** To structure an idea into a clear, concise concept.
**Concept Card – The AQUA Ecosystem**

| Field | Response |
|---|---|
| **1. Problem** | Rural aquaculture farmers lose massive profits due to unpredictable diseases and exploitative middlemen, and existing tech is too complex and English-centric. |
| **2. Solution** | **The AQUA Platform:** A vernacular, AI-powered ecosystem combining real-time pond health predictions with a direct trade marketplace. |
| **3. Key Benefit** | Increases farmer net income by preventing diseases early and connecting them directly to premium buyers. |
| **4. Differentiator** | Highly localized (regional languages) and provides end-to-end support (from seed selection to final harvest sale), unlike fragmented single-feature apps. |
| **5. Target User** | Small-to-medium aquaculture farmers in rural areas. |
| **6. Tagline / Name** | **AQUA** – "Predict. Protect. Prosper." |
| **7. Sketch / Visual** | *(A mobile app screen showing a large green shield icon reading "Pond Health: Excellent", a prominent camera button for "Scan Fish", and a "Sell Harvest" button at the bottom—all text displayed in the local regional language).* |

## Worksheet 3: Storyboard Template
**Purpose:** To visualize how the user interacts with your concept from problem to transformation.
**Storyboard – The Journey of a Farmer on AQUA**

| Frame | What Happens Here | Description / Sketch Space |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | The user’s problem moment | **Farmer Ramesh notices dying fish.** He sees several fish floating near the surface but doesn't know the exact disease. He fears a massive financial loss. |
| **2** | User’s frustration or challenge | **He feels helpless.** The local expert is miles away, and he cannot afford to wait days for a lab diagnosis. |
| **3** | Discovery/intro of concept | **He opens the AQUA app.** He selects his local language and clicks the large "AI Vision Diagnostic" camera button. |
| **4** | First experience using it | **He takes a photo of the affected fish.** The app instantly processes the image and identifies "Fin Rot" with 95% confidence. |
| **5** | The transformation | **The app provides an exact, low-cost treatment plan** (e.g., "Apply 5kg of specific probiotic"). Ramesh acts immediately, and within days, the pond recovers. |
| **6** | Final emotional outcome | **Fast forward to harvest time.** Ramesh clicks "List for Sale" on the AQUA Direct Trade Marketplace, gets a bid 20% higher than local middlemen, and smiles, securing his family's livelihood. |

## Worksheet 4: Reflection Sheet
**Purpose:** To help participants capture key insights and personal takeaways.
**Reflection Worksheet – My Design Thinking Experience**

1. **My biggest learning or “aha” moment today:** 
   True innovation in agritech isn't just about having the most advanced AI; it's about accessibility, language localization, and removing friction for a low-literacy user.

2. **The idea or concept I’m most proud of:** 
   Combining predictive disease AI with a direct B2B marketplace in one unified ecosystem, solving both the technical and economic problems farmers face simultaneously.

3. **The ideation technique that helped me most (circle one):** 
   **SCAMPER** *(specifically the 'Combine' and 'Eliminate' prompts helped me rethink the supply chain).*

4. **If I had one more day, I would:** 
   Interview three local middlemen and three exporters to test the viability and friction points of the Direct Trade Marketplace feature.

5. **What this exercise taught me about creativity:** 
   Creativity is often about connecting two existing but separate solutions (scientific farming advice + e-commerce) to solve a larger, systemic problem.

6. **My next step to develop my idea:** 
   Map out the exact UI/UX wireframes to ensure a farmer can successfully upload a photo for AI analysis in 3 clicks or less.
